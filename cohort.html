The rhythm

One month. Four movements.

Ground

Name what AI is actually changing in your role, your work, and your sense of value. Emotional awareness before tool adoption.

Build

Hands-on sessions where you make something real with AI — workflows, prototypes, experiments you take back into your work.

Question

Ethics woven into the practice, not bolted on. What should this tool do? What shouldn't it? Where do you draw your lines?

Lead

Form a point of view you can stand behind — and practice leading the AI conversation in your team, company, or community.

Who it's for & what you leave with

Thoughtful people in the middle of the shift.

Professionals, founders, coaches, teachers, creators, and leaders who are curious and a little overwhelmed — and who want the practical and the human side of this change, together.

You'll build

Working AI experiments in your own context — not demo-ware. Something you use the Monday after.

You'll understand

What's actually happening under the hype — enough technical grounding to be credible, without the jargon.

You'll belong

A peer group that keeps meeting after the month ends. The community is the product.

Questions

Answered honestly.

Do I need a technical background?

No. The cohort is designed for thoughtful professionals, not engineers. You'll get enough technical grounding to be credible and hands-on, without jargon or prerequisites.

How much time does it take?

One live session per week plus small experiments you run inside your actual work — the practice is designed to fit into your job, not compete with it.

What if I miss a session?

Sessions build on each other, but life happens. You'll get notes and a way to catch up with your peer circle before the next session.

Is this an AI training?

No. You will genuinely build with AI — that part is real and hands-on. But trainings transfer skills and end. This is a leadership practice: identity, ethics, impact, and a community that keeps asking the questions with you after the month is over.

The InsightsOut cohort · Small group · Monthly

Group coaching for leaders in the middle of change.

A month of guided group coaching with peers navigating the same shift. We make sense of what's changing, process the hard parts honestly, and practice leading through it — so you leave clearer, steadier, and ready to lead your people. Not a course. Not a lecture. A room.

The rhythm

One month. Four movements.

Ground

Name what's actually changing — in your role, your work, and your sense of value. You can't lead what you haven't named.

Make sense

Sort signal from noise, together. What does this change mean for you, your team, your next six months?

Process

Space for the real feelings — fear, frustration, grief, excitement — so they inform your leadership instead of quietly driving it.

Lead

Form a point of view you can stand behind — and practice the conversations and decisions that carry your people through.

Who it's for & what you leave with

Thoughtful people in the middle of the shift.

Founders, managers, professionals, coaches, and teachers whose work is changing under them — and who want to face it with other people instead of alone.

You'll get clear

What's changing, what it means for you, and what you actually want to do about it.

You'll get steady

Less overwhelm, more ground. The feelings get processed instead of carried.

You'll belong

A peer group that keeps meeting after the month ends. Nobody leads change alone here.

Questions

Answered honestly.

Do I need a technical background?

Not at all. This is about leading through change, not about technology. No prerequisites, no jargon.

How much time does it take?

One live session per week plus small experiments you run inside your actual work — the practice is designed to fit into your job, not compete with it.

What if I miss a session?

Sessions build on each other, but life happens. You'll get notes and a way to catch up with your peer circle before the next session.

Is this an AI course?

No. It's group coaching for leadership in a time of rapid change. AI comes up constantly — it's driving much of the change — but the work here is sensemaking, emotions, decisions, and leading people. If you want tool training, this isn't it.

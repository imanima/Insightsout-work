Why AI adoption stalls

A rollout is not the same as real change.

AI plans are often set at the top and training is delivered away from daily work. People are then expected to change before they have enough context, practice, or support.

Change is done to people

Teams are told to adopt AI before they can help shape how it should support their work.

Training stops at awareness

People learn what the tools can do but do not get enough practice applying them to their own roles and workflows.

Managers are left to carry it

Managers must answer questions, support emotions, set expectations, and keep work moving without a clear approach.

How the Lab works

Start with one workflow that matters.

A focused engagement that moves one important piece of work from an idea to tested use, clear ownership, and follow through.

Choose the right work

Start with a real business need and the people who understand the work best.

Make the handoff

Decide what people own, where AI helps, and where human review is needed.

Train through real work

Build the relevant skills while the team practices with its own tasks, decisions, and quality standards.

Improve and expand

See what people use, fix what gets in the way, and decide what should happen next.

A useful place to start

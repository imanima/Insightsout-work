# InsightsOut Upcoming Events

## Complete Luma creation and launch guide

Prepared July 2026

This is the working guide for creating the first InsightsOut public events on Luma. It includes the calendar setup, launch sequence, shared event settings, registration questions, confirmation and reminder copy, and complete listings for all five events.

The goal is not to fill a calendar with disconnected workshops. Each event is a clear front door for one audience. Every participant should understand what the session is, what they will get, and how they can continue with InsightsOut afterward.

## 1. The launch decision

Create all five events in Luma as private events first. Publish only the first two after their date, place, price, and facilitation capacity are confirmed.

### Publish first

1. **My Team Is Overwhelmed. How Do I Lead?**
2. **From AI Overwhelm to Focus**

These are the strongest first events because they reach two clear audiences with an immediate problem and a natural paid next step.

The manager event can lead to manager community, the Leadership Circle, and organizational work. The founder event can lead to Founder Clarity, the Leadership Circle, and private coaching.

### Keep private until the first dates are working

3. **What Should Stay Human?**
4. **My Role Is Changing. What's My Next Move?**
5. **I Don't Know Where to Start With AI. Start Here.**

Do not publish all five at once. A smaller, active calendar feels more credible than a large calendar with weak attendance.

## 2. Recommended rhythm

Run two public events each month. Repeat strong events instead of inventing a new event every week.

| Month | First event | Second event |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Month 1 | My Team Is Overwhelmed. How Do I Lead? | From AI Overwhelm to Focus |
| Month 2 | What Should Stay Human? | My Role Is Changing. What's My Next Move? |
| Month 3 | Repeat the manager event | I Don't Know Where to Start With AI. Start Here. |
| Month 4 | Repeat the best converting audience event | What Should Stay Human? |

Use the results from the first three months to decide which audience event becomes monthly. Keep **What Should Stay Human?** as the shared gathering that brings different audiences together every six to eight weeks.

## 5. The InsightsOut experience

Every event should feel different because the audience and problem are different. The underlying method should remain consistent.

### Public explanation

Use this short line when the event needs an explanation of the format:

> Bring one real situation. We will make sense of it together, clarify what matters, and choose one useful next step. This is a working session, not a webinar.

### Shared 90 minute shape

| Time | Purpose | Participant output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 to 10 minutes | Welcome, agreements, and choose one real situation | A real question to work on |
| 10 to 25 minutes | Name what is happening without rushing to fix it | The pressure or tension underneath the surface |
| 25 to 45 minutes | Listen and make sense of the situation with peers | A clearer view and a better question |
| 45 to 65 minutes | Use one practical framework | What matters, what can be shaped, and what stays human |
| 65 to 82 minutes | Choose and prepare a next move | A decision, conversation, experiment, or action |
| 82 to 90 minutes | Commit, connect, and close | One action and a way to continue |

### Room agreements

Open every event with these agreements:

1. Stories stay. Learning can leave.
2. Sharing is always optional.
3. Speak from your own experience.
4. We are here to understand before we advise.
5. No one needs to perform certainty.
6. Leave with one action small enough to complete.

Luma already collects name and email. Add only questions that improve the room or make the follow up more relevant.

Set all registration questions before sharing the event. Luma does not apply new questions retroactively to people who already registered.

### Questions used on every event

Required: No

Yes, send me occasional InsightsOut updates about future events, community gatherings, research, and new programs. I can unsubscribe at any time.

Question type: Checkbox

Required: No

**Optional anonymous learning consent**

InsightsOut studies patterns in how people experience rapid change. May we combine your anonymous registration response and workshop feedback with other participants' responses to improve our programs and share general findings? We will not publish your name, company, story, or words without asking you separately.

Question type: Checkbox

Required: No

### Important research rule

Attending an event does not mean someone has consented to research, a testimonial, photography, or marketing.

Keep these permissions separate:

1. Event registration
2. Mailing list permission
3. Anonymous learning and research permission
4. Photo or recording permission
5. Permission to use an identifiable quote or story

Ask separately before publishing a person's name, image, company, quote, or identifiable story.

**Subject**

What became clearer?

**Message**

> Thank you for what you brought into the room today. What became clearer? What will you do next? Your feedback helps us make the next session more useful. If you give us permission, anonymous patterns may also inform InsightsOut research on rapid change, work, leadership, and AI adoption.

Do not use a positive comment as a testimonial unless the participant gives separate permission.

## 13. How to create each event in Luma

Use Luma on the web because the advanced Blast editor and scheduling controls are available there.

### Step 1: Create the event shell

1. Go to https://luma.com/create.
2. Select the **InsightsOut** calendar.
3. Enter the event title exactly as written in this guide.
4. Add the confirmed date, start time, end time, and time zone.
5. Choose one event mode: in person or online.
6. Add the venue or meeting link.
7. Upload the square event cover.
8. Paste the full event description.
9. Set visibility to **Private** while building.
10. Create the event.

### Step 2: Set registration

1. Open **Manage Event**.
2. Go to **Registration**.
3. Open the ticket type.
4. Name and price the ticket using the event recommendation.
5. Keep **Require Approval** off for the public pilot.
6. Set the total ticket capacity.
7. Turn on the waitlist.
8. Set ticket sales to end two hours before the event.
9. Add the event specific registration questions.
10. Add the shared access and consent questions.
11. Add the custom confirmation email.

### Step 3: Add hosts and presentation

1. Confirm that the event says **Presented by InsightsOut**.
2. Add Nima Imani as a visible host.
3. Check the host photo and bio.
4. Add a facilitator or partner only when they are confirmed.
5. Give event management access only to people who need it.

### Step 4: Set communications

1. Open the **Blasts** tab.
2. Schedule the event specific preparation message for 24 hours before the event.
3. Keep Luma's normal reminder enabled.
4. Schedule the post event feedback request for two hours after the event.
5. Prepare the event specific follow up email and send it within 24 hours after the event.

Luma Blasts can be scheduled and sent to guests by status or ticket type. Use the advanced editor on the web for longer messages.

## 15. What to measure

The first events are not only attendance opportunities. They are tests of the offer and sources of learning.

Track these numbers for each event:

1. Event page views
2. Registrations
3. Registration conversion rate
4. Source of registration
5. Attendance rate
6. Waitlist size
7. Feedback response rate
8. Average feedback rating
9. Number who follow the calendar or opt into updates
10. Number who take the intended next step
11. Number of organization or coaching conversations
12. Number of paid conversions within 30 days

Also capture these qualitative patterns:

1. The words people use to describe the problem
2. The situation they brought
3. What became clearer
4. The action they chose
5. What they wanted next
6. Which parts of the listing attracted them

Only include responses in research when the participant has given the appropriate consent. Remove identifying details before combining responses into themes.

## 16. Post event operations

Within 24 hours after each event:

1. Send the event specific follow up.
2. Export the guest list and registration answers.
3. Record attendance and source data.
4. Review anonymous feedback.
5. Ask selected participants separately for testimonial permission.
6. Tag relevant contacts by audience: manager, People leader, founder, role change, independent professional, partner.
7. Send the correct next step, not every InsightsOut offer.
8. Record repeated language and themes for future articles and research.
9. Debrief the facilitation while the room is still fresh.
10. Decide what to keep, change, or remove before cloning the event.

## 17. Clone successful events

Once the first version is working, clone it rather than recreating it. Luma copies the description, location, ticket types, and settings, but not the guest list or event Blasts. After cloning:

1. Change the date and time.
2. Check the venue.
3. Recheck the registration close time.
4. Schedule the preparation Blast again.
5. Schedule feedback again.
6. Update any copy based on what participants actually said.
7. Test the new link before publishing.
